## 4. Valuation and re-rate math

**Illustrative, assumption-driven — not forecasts.** Project level: 192 MW at ~30–35% capacity factor ≈ 505–590 GWh/yr; at GEA-2 wind tariffs (~₱5.8–6.4/kWh, 20-yr contracts) that is roughly **₱3.0–3.5B/yr project revenue** vs the company's entire FY2025 revenue of ₱360M — a step-change of ~9x at project level (Alternergy's economic share is smaller: 60% of Tanay, Alabat assumed 100%). Wind EBITDA margins of 75–80% then service the construction debt that today scares the market. That is the whole trade: **the same leverage number flips from threat to torque on COD day.**

| Scenario | Assumptions | Implied price | On 500k sh |
|---|---|---|---:|
| Bear | COD slips past mid-2027, pref offer fails, common raise below book | 0.6–0.7× BV → **₱0.55–0.64** | ₱275k–320k |
| Base | Both farms at COD by mid-2027, first full contracted year 2027–28, market pays ~1.0–1.2× a growing book | **₱0.95–1.15** | ₱475k–575k |
| Bull | Clean COD 2026, pref funds pipeline, 2028–30 build-out visible, market pays growth-IPP multiples (1.5×+ BV) | **₱1.40+** | ₱700k+ |

At ₱0.79 you are being paid to wait for base case. Bear case requires *two* independent failures (schedule + funding). That asymmetry is the position.

## 6. Risk map and tripwires

Each risk has a **tripwire** (objective, checkable) and a **response**. No vibes.

1. **Schedule risk** — Tanay already slipped once; typhoon season can push commissioning.
   *Tripwire:* No Alabat COD by **end-Q4 2026**, or no Tanay COD by **mid-2027**. *Response:* formal re-underwrite; freeze new buys above ₱0.75 until resolved.
2. **Funding pivot risk** — pref offer fails; management raises **common** below book.
   *Tripwire:* Any announced common issuance/SRO below ₱0.91. *Response:* this falsifies a thesis pillar — halt accumulation immediately, reassess position size.
3. **Pref stack drag** — ₱5B at, say, 7–8.5% = ₱350–425M/yr senior to common.
   *Tripwire:* Coupon prices > ~8.5%, or issue size exceeds ₱5B shelf. *Response:* trim base-case scenario, extend re-rate timeline; not an exit event.
4. **Generation shortfall** — wind resource below P50; curtailment on the grid link.
   *Tripwire:* First 12 months' capacity factor persistently < ~28% or material disclosed curtailment. *Response:* re-underwrite; contracted tariff only pays on delivered energy.
5. **Typhoon tail risk** — Alabat is deliberately on the Pacific coastline; that is both the resource and the exposure.
   *Tripwire:* Direct major-typhoon hit with disclosed structural damage. *Response:* check insurance/business-interruption disclosure before reacting to the price gap; the market will overreact — that may be a buy, not a sell.
6. **Liquidity reality** — ALTER trades thin. 500k shares exits in days, not minutes, and not at the mid.
   *Standing rule:* exits, if ever, are worked patiently; never market-dump a tripwire reaction.
7. **Anchor risk** — RCR is office-heavy in a soft PH office market, offset by sponsor infusions and rising DPS.
   *Tripwire:* Any quarterly DPS cut below ₱0.10. *Response:* loop pauses; anchor itself goes under review.
8. **Rate environment** — BSP easing lifts both legs (REIT yield spread + refinancing cost); tightening squeezes both. Monitor, no tripwire — it's cycle, not thesis.
